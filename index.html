Frequently asked questions

Do I need to do anything to prepare for my massage?

We advise you to prepare for your massage session by ensuring proper hydration, refraining from consuming heavy meals, and dressing in comfortable attire.

Is a monthly massage essential?

Scheduling a massage therapy session once a month is beneficial for preventative care or stress management. For addressing an injury or chronic tension, we suggest trying to receive treatment on a weekly basis.

What are the various massage options you offer?

Our company offers the following types of massages: AcuPressure, Deep Tissue, Sports Recovery, Thai Massage, Swedish Massage, Couples Massage, Shiatsu Massage, and Lymphatic Drainage. Furthermore, our highly trained and certified therapists are able to customize and tailor each session to meet your specific needs and preferences, including any massages not listed on our menu.

What is the process for canceling a scheduled session?

In order to ensure the most seamless cancellation process, we kindly request that you provide us with at least 24 hours notice prior to your scheduled appointment. This allows us the opportunity to offer the appointment time to another client and to assist you in rescheduling for a more convenient date and time that better aligns with your schedule.

Is it safe to get a massage during pregnancy?

It is generally considered safe to receive massage therapy during pregnancy, but it is important to consult with your healthcare provider first. Massage during pregnancy can help with many common pregnancy discomforts such as low back pain, swelling, and fatigue, as well as promote relaxation and overall well-being for both the mother and the baby.

Is it necessary to disrobe for my massage?

No, it is not necessary to disrobe for your massage. The amount of clothing worn during your massage is left up to your comfort level. The therapist will work with you to ensure your modesty is maintained and only exposed areas that are being massaged will be uncovered. It is important to communicate with your therapist regarding your preferred level of clothing and draping during the massage.
